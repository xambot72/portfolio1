Developed for SuperStudio 2024, an architecture competition run by SONA, this project responds to the growing need for resilient, rapidly deployable housing in the wake of extreme weather events. Designed for remote regions above the Tropic of Capricorn, the modular system accommodates 100 displaced individuals while withstanding bushfires, floods, and cyclones with minimal recovery requirements. Prioritizing ease of assembly, scalability, and cost-effectiveness, the design ensures deployment within two weeks and provides stable housing for at least six months. By leveraging readily available materials and modular construction, this approach delivers an adaptable, long-term solution for disaster relief.

The proposal incorporates standard scaffold bracings and couplers to form a structural exoskeleton. This frames a modular 2500x3000 grid that serves as the foundation for individual dwellings, with the flexibility to expand into larger units for families or communal spaces.
